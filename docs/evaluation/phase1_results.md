# Overtime for IBEW Generation Electricians (2025-2030)

Overtime for IBEW Generation electricians is defined based on the work schedule in place. The primary overtime provision applies to the standard eight (8) hour day, five (5) day per week schedule:

## Standard Schedule (Monday-Friday, 8-hour days):

**Hours beyond the normal daily scheduled number up to 2 hours per day are paid at 1.5 times the basic hourly rate. All hours exceeding 10 hours per day are paid at 2 times the base hourly rate.**

> "When working on an eight (8) hour day and five (5) day per week work schedule (Monday to Friday inclusive), overtime work shall be paid at one and one-half (1 ½) times the basic hourly rate for all hours worked beyond the normal daily scheduled number of hours up to a maximum of two (2) hours per day. All hours in excess of 10 hours per day shall be paid at two (2) times the base hourly rate." [SOURCE 6, Section 806 A]

## Additional Requirements:

- **Voluntary Basis**: "All overtime shall be worked on a voluntary basis and shall be distributed as equally as possible among all IBEW members." [SOURCE 1, Page 24]

- **Rest Periods Before Overtime**: "For employees required to work overtime, a ten (10) minute rest period will be allotted prior to the end of the normal shift before commencing overtime work." [SOURCE 2, Section 802 B]

- **Rest Periods During Overtime**: "For employees working overtime, a fifteen (15) minute rest period will be allotted, at a time directed by the Employer, after each two hours of overtime worked." [SOURCE 2, Section 802 C]

The agreement also contemplates other shift arrangements (one, two, or three-shift operations), which may have different overtime calculations, though those specific provisions are not detailed in the provided sources.

The regular daily hours of work for Sheet Metal workers depend on the work schedule established by the Employer and are **not fixed to a single daily amount**. Instead, the agreement provides flexibility:

### Overtime Meal Provisions

**[SOURCE 5 / SOURCE 7] — Section 806B, Page 24:**

- If **not notified the previous day** that more than 2 hours of overtime is required: after approximately 2 hours worked, the employee receives a **lunch break of 30 minutes paid at base rate**; after each additional 4 hours, another 30-minute paid meal break is provided. If a hot meal cannot be supplied, the Employer pays **$25.00**.
- If **notified the previous day**: no lunch is provided, but a **30-minute paid meal break** is still allowed.
- When a paid meal period overlaps a rest period, the **paid meal period supplants the rest period**.
- These provisions **do not apply to the first eight (8) hours worked on Saturdays, Sundays, and Recognized Holidays**.
